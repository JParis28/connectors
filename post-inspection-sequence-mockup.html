Subject: Good news about your roof

{{firstName}},

Your roof checked out clean — no damage, no immediate concerns. That's the best kind of inspection report to get.

A few things to keep on your radar for maintenance:

Gutters: Clean them twice a year (spring and fall). Clogged gutters back up water under your shingles.

Tree branches: Keep anything hanging within 6 feet of your roof trimmed back. Branches scrape shingles and drop debris.

Attic ventilation: If your attic feels hot in summer or you see condensation in winter, your ventilation might need a look. Poor airflow shortens roof life by years.

Your roof has plenty of life left. We'd recommend another inspection in 2–3 years, or sooner if a major storm comes through.

If anything comes up before then, you've got our number.
